Research & Publications

Academic Research

Temporal Indexing

TIDE-Extension

A multi-version temporal database index built on a two-tier architecture: a Key-Tree at the top layer, with per-key-range TIDE + Alive List structures at the bottom, using adaptive splitting. Implemented in Rust on the indie-histree codebase, with the MVB-tree as the primary performance benchmark. Designed for workloads requiring efficient point-in-time and interval queries over versioned data.

Applicable to: government records systems, financial audit trails, and any platform where querying historical state at a precise point in time is a compliance or operational requirement.

Vector Search

TACO-GANN

Temporal and Category-aware Approximate Nearest Neighbour Search — a vector index that supports simultaneous timestamp and categorical pre-filtering before similarity scoring. Implemented in Python, TACO-GANN is directly applicable to RAG pipelines, recommendation engines, and any domain where "when" and "what type" constrain the semantic search space. The two systems are complementary and independently deployable.

Applicable to: RAG pipelines, recommendation engines, and enterprise search where time and category constraints must filter before semantic scoring.
