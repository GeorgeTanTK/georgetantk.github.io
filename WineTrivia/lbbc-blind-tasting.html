🍷 LBBC Blind Tasting Checklist

Your personal mental flowchart for competition day

Red Wine — Identify AOC First
1First Nose (2 seconds)
Do you smell "kolapis" (mineral / iodine / saline / metallic)?
YES → Go to Step 2
NO → Skip to Step 3
2Kolapis Detected — Which Type?
Flowery + perfumy + red fruit?
→ MARGAUX
Smoky + flinty + warm earth?
→ PESSAC-LÉOGNAN
Graphite + cedar + powerful tannin?
→ PAUILLAC
None of the above? Sip it — watery/dilute on palate?
→ HAUT-MÉDOC
💡 If kolapis but can't differentiate: check tannin — muscular/fine = Pauillac, rustic/coarse = Haut-Médoc, smooth/earthy = Pessac
3No Kolapis — St-Julien or St-Estèphe?
Red + black fruit, balanced, harmonious, "everything fits"?
Tannin feels warm/integrated
→ ST-JULIEN
Black fruit, austere, reserved, stern?
Tannin feels COLD/gripping
→ ST-ESTÈPHE
💡 The KEY: tannin temperature. St-Estèphe = cold. St-Julien = warm. Ask yourself: does this feel stern or harmonious?
4Confirm on Palate

Red Wine — Identify Vintage (After AOC)
1Is It a Weak or Strong Vintage?
First impression: is there green / herbaceous / hollow character?
YES → Weak cluster (Step 2)
NO → Good-to-great cluster (Step 3)
2Weak Vintage — Which One?
Very green + watery + NO fruit at all?
→ 2013 (worst modern vintage)
Green tannin but SOME black fruit pushing through?
→ 2011 (underripe, not empty)
Fresh, herbal, PEPPERY / STALKY, lean?
→ 2017 (frost year — lean not hollow)
Herbaceous + rough tannin but not totally hollow?
→ 2012 (mediocre, not terrible)
3Good-to-Great Vintage — Which One?
DUSTY / monumental / iron-fist tannin?
→ 2010
RIPE / soft / round tannin + fleshy fruit?
→ 2009
MUSCULAR tannin + floral lift + silky?
→ 2016 (hunt for florals!)
JUICY + peppery + generous fruit?
→ 2015
JAMMY + high tannin + hot/overripe feeling?
→ 2020 or 2018
CIGAR / tobacco + fresh fruit + structured?
→ 2014 (Indian summer)
EARTHY / mitti + moderate tannin + long finish?
→ 2008 (classic claret)
4Hardest Calls — Differentiators

2015 vs 2016

2015: Juicy, generous, peppery, fruit-forward
2016: More structured, tannin frame dominates, FLORAL LIFT beneath

2011 vs 2017

2011: Green tannin + some black fruit trying to emerge
2017: Peppery/stalky specifically, feels LEAN + FRESH not just green

2009 vs 2010

2009: Soft, round, hedonistic, fleshy pleasure
2010: Dusty, monumental, iron-fist power, more angular

2008 vs 2014

2008: Mitti/earthy, salty, red fruit, classic feel
2014: Cigar/tobacco, fresh fruit, structured (Indian summer)

Sweet Wine — Identify AOC
1First Nose — What Dominates?
LEMON / CITRUS / SOUR dominant?
→ Likely BARSAC
RAISIN / HONEY / SWEET dominant?
→ Likely SAUTERNES
2Confirm on Palate (CRITICAL for traps)
High acidity + LIGHTER body + LINEAR finish?
→ BARSAC ✅ (even if nose smelled rich)
Viscous / STICKY + HEAVIER body + ROUND finish?
→ SAUTERNES ✅ (even if nose had citrus)
💡 GOLDEN RULE: When nose and palate disagree, TRUST THE PALATE for AOC. The nose can mislead (Climens!), the palate rarely lies.
3Sweet Wine Vintage Signals
Rich, dense, oaky, long finish?
→ Great botrytis year (2010, 2015, 2016)
Fresh, fruity, lighter, honey?
→ Decent year or younger wine
High sour, watery, short finish?
→ Weak botrytis (2012, 2017)
Oaky, bitter, not much fruit?
→ Failed/marginal (2002, 2003)
Tertiary Notes — Age Detector
13-Layer Nose Check (Do This in Order)

Pass 1 — Primary (fruit):

Fresh red/black fruit? Flowers? Fresh herbs? → YOUNG

Pass 2 — Secondary (winemaking):

Vanilla? Toast? Chocolate? Coffee? Coconut? → OAK INFLUENCE

Pass 3 — Tertiary (age): IGNORE FRUIT, hunt for:

Dried fruit (prune, fig, dried cherry)?
Earthy (forest floor, mushroom, mitti, wet leaves)?
Old wood (cigar box, cedar chest, old library)?
Savoury (leather, cured meat, game, broth)?
Spicy (tobacco leaf, black tea, dried herbs)?
Oxidative hints (soy, balsamic, nuts)?
If ≥2 tertiary boxes checked → Wine is likely pre-2012
If 0-1 tertiary boxes → Wine is likely 2013 or later
2Sweet Wine Tertiary Markers
Dried apricot, candied orange, marmalade, quince paste?
Nutty: almond, hazelnut, walnut, praline?
Caramelised: toffee, crème brûlée, butterscotch?
Waxy: beeswax, lanolin, honeycomb?
Evolved botrytis: gingerbread, barley sugar, saffron, curry spice?
💡 On palate: aged sweet wines feel less overtly sugary, more savoury-nutty, and texture becomes oily/waxy rather than just sticky
3Track Evolution (5-10 min)
What HappensMeaning
Primary fades → leather/earth/tobaccoAged / mature wine
Tight nose opens → floral/perfumeYoung fine wine needing time
Green burns off → fruit emergesDecent vintage, good terroir
Nothing changesSimple wine / weak vintage

⚠️ Trap Wines — Don't Get Caught
!Climens — Barsac Disguised as Sauternes

What happens: Nose smells rich, honeyed, powerful — like Sauternes

Why: 100% Sémillon (only estate in Barsac) → extra richness on nose

Your anchor: Smells like Sauternes but finishes sour/fresh on swallow → BARSAC (Climens)
You noted this yourself: "sour and fresh when swallow"
!Guiraud — Smoky Sauternes (Like Climens?)

What happens: Oaky, smoky character — you wrote "comparable with Climens"

Why: Organic estate with distinctive winemaking style

Your anchor: Smoky BUT rich and viscous on palate → SAUTERNES (Guiraud)
You noted: "dry fruit, more ripeness and richness"
!Giscours — Margaux That Isn't Margaux-like

What happens: Mineral, masculine, no perfume — feels like St-Estèphe

Why: Southernmost Margaux, heavier soils than typical appellation

Your anchor: If a "Margaux" has no flowers but has kolapis/mineral → think Giscours
!Palmer — Left Bank With Right Bank Character

What happens: Rounder, softer, more Merlot-driven than expected for Margaux

Why: ~50% Merlot — uniquely high for classified Left Bank

Your anchor: If a Margaux feels unusually round/fleshy → think Palmer
